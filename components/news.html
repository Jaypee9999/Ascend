CHED Partnership July 10, 2025

CHED Eyes Support for Groundbreaking Academe-Industry Linkage

Mr. Albert Samuela (Middle left) and Ms. Geraldine Buela (Middle right), together with student interns working on the ASCEND project, met with representatives from the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) Technical Panel for Chemistry and the Office of Programs and Standards Development (OPSD) at UP Diliman on July 10, 2025.

The meeting centered on presenting the ASCEND project—a proposed database platform that bridges academic institutions and the chemical industry...

The meeting centered on presenting the ASCEND project—a proposed database platform that bridges academic institutions and the chemical industry. Based on the meeting, the CHED Technical Panel for Chemistry expressed openness to supporting an academic-industry linkage project aimed at strengthening ties between higher education institutions and the chemical industry.

JP Dela Cruz from CHED shared that projects like ASCEND, a database platform that connects the two sectors, could gain the panel's interest, especially if it helps align research with industry needs. CHED encourages the project to be proposed in collaboration with the technical panel and include its benefits to both CHED and higher education institutions (HEIs) for possible endorsement and funding. A "pilot implementation" is advised as the first step to move the project forward.

The platform will only feature public information from HEIs and chemical companies, while detailed content will be protected as intellectual property. CHED sees no issue with private organizations leading the project, as long as plans are clear. To simplify the process, CHED recommends starting with Chemistry before involving Chemical Engineering, as the latter's panel is still being reformed. The project will also identify how industry partners contribute— through funding, internships, or jobs—to help revise and improve the chemistry curriculum in line with real-world demands.

ASCEND Team Engages with UP Diliman to Foster Industry-Academic Innovation Partnership

On July 10, 2025 - The ASCEND Team held a virtual meeting with representatives from the University of the Philippines Diliman (UPD) to advance their collaborative efforts under the flagship initiative of the Board of Investments' Academe-Industry Matching (AIM!) Program.

This landmark event involved ASEAN Engr. Albert Samuela and BOI's Ms. Geraldine Buela, together with interns from Nalco...

This landmark event involved ASEAN Engr. Albert Samuela and BOI's Ms. Geraldine Buela, together with interns from Nalco, who communicated with UPD Chemical Engineering Department's Industry Linkage Officer and Associate Professor Julie Anne D.R. Paraggua to solidify the industrial-academic linkage project.

During the virtual meeting, Associate Professor Paraggua shared valuable insights and advice - she underscored the university's commitment to aligning research and education with dynamic industry demands, improving the program to make it more in line with the goals and what appeals for potential partners for the projects.

Both parties discussed ASCEND's core mission to co-create solutions on linking industry expertise with academic research. Through such partnerships, ASCEND aims to cultivate a robust talent pipeline for the chemicals sector, foster the commercialization of research, and reinforce the Philippines' position as a hub for chemical innovation and industrial progress.

DLSU Partnership July 8, 2025

Strategic Meeting with De La Salle University Engineering Dean

MANILA, Philippines – ASEAN Engr. Albert Samuela (Top Middle) along with interns from SPIK (Samahan sa Pilipinas ng mga Industriyang Kimika), in conjunction with the Board of Investments (BOI), held a crucial online meeting with Dr. Kathleen Aviso (Top Right), Dean of the Gokongwei College of Engineering at De La Salle University, on July 8, 2025.

The virtual session, conducted via Microsoft Teams, aimed to strategize and seek expert guidance on fostering stronger industry-academe collaboration...

The virtual session, conducted via Microsoft Teams, aimed to strategize and seek expert guidance on fostering stronger industry-academe collaboration for university research projects.

The discussion focused on establishing effective procedures for partnerships before, during, and after collaborative research endeavors. With Dr. Aviso's extensive background in Process Systems Engineering and her advocacy for sustainable industrial systems, the participants sought her insights to streamline the process of engaging with industry partners and maximizing the impact of academic research.

The initiative reflects a growing recognition of the vital role that robust linkages between educational institutions and industries play in national development and innovation.

UPLB-TTBDO Recent

ASCEND Project Taps UPLB-TTBDO for Best Practices in Linking Student Research with Industry

The representative of the ASCEND project, Albert Samuela, recently met with the University of the Philippines Los Baños Technology Transfer and Business Development Office (UPLB-TTBDO), led by Director Catherine Amante, to explore best practices in linking student research with industry partners.

The discussion focused on UPLB-TTBDO's systematic approach to facilitating collaborations, including intellectual property management...

The discussion focused on UPLB-TTBDO's systematic approach to facilitating collaborations, including intellectual property management, commercialization strategies, and stakeholder engagement. Samuela sought insights to strengthen the ASCEND Project, which aims to bridge higher education institutions (HEIs) with industry players to enhance innovation and workforce readiness.

The meeting highlighted UPLB-TTBDO's success in transforming student theses and research into market-ready solutions through structured partnerships. Amante shared key steps, such as identifying industry needs, securing agreements, and providing mentorship to researchers.

Samuela emphasized that these strategies will serve as a model for ASCEND's mission to foster sustainable HEI-industry ties. "By learning from established programs like UPLB-TTBDO, we can create a more robust framework for nationwide collaboration," he said. The dialogue marks a significant step in aligning academic research with real-world industry demands.

The conversation centered on DOST-ITDI's structured approach to collaborating with industry partners, including needs assessment, joint R&D initiatives, and technology transfer processes. Samuela gathered insights to refine the ASCEND Project, which seeks to strengthen ties between higher education institutions (HEIs) and industries to drive innovation and workforce development.

During the meeting, Esguerra highlighted DOST-ITDI's role in bridging research outputs with real-world applications, ensuring that industry needs are met through targeted scientific and technological solutions.

Samuela emphasized that these best practices will serve as a foundation for ASCEND's framework, enabling HEIs to better tailor research projects to industry requirements. "By learning from DOST-ITDI's expertise, we can create more effective pathways for academic-industry partnerships," he said. The discussion marks a crucial step in fostering stronger collaboration between research institutions and the private sector.
